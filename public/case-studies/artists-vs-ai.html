Context & People

Placeholder image for context and people

This research explored how small content creators experience algorithmic bias, visibility uncertainty, and platform opacity. Stakeholders included independent artists and creators whose income and exposure depend on algorithmic systems.

A key pain point was the lack of transparency and perceived unfairness in AI-driven content distribution.

My Method & Rationale

Placeholder image for method and rationale

I conducted mixed-method research combining qualitative analysis of creator experiences with evaluation of AI auditing tools. This approach allowed me to capture both subjective trust perceptions and broader behavioral patterns.

The goal was to produce actionable insights rather than purely theoretical critiques of AI bias.

Key Findings & Impact

Placeholder image for findings and impact

Findings revealed that creators experienced emotional stress and strategic uncertainty due to opaque algorithm behavior. Clearer feedback systems and transparency mechanisms were identified as key UX opportunities.

The project resulted in business and product recommendations aimed at improving trust, fairness perception, and platform usability for smaller creators.

This project deepened my understanding of AI ethics, user trust, and platform accountability. I improved my mixed-method analysis and stakeholder communication skills.
