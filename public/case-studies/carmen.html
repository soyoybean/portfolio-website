CARMEN (Cognitively Assistive Robot for Motivation & Neurorehabilitation)

Cognitively assistive robot for home-based neurorehabilitation and motivation

My Role
Co-design & Participatory research
Team
3 Researchers, 2 Designers
Timeline
Aug 2020 – May 2023

Summary

Translated clinical interventions into robot interaction design, enabling collaborative goal-setting, personalization, and sustained engagement in real-world care.

Context & People

CARMEN supports people with Mild Cognitive Impairment (PwMCI) through home-based neurorehabilitation.

A core challenge was translating clinician-led cognitive training into autonomous robot interactions that motivate without becoming intrusive, controlling, or patronizing in intimate home environments.

Existing research provides limited guidance on how to adapt clinical interventions for long-term, real-world deployment.

My Contribution

  • Led live co-design with PwMCI, caregivers, and clinicians to translate clinical practices into robot interaction behaviors
  • Conducted interviews and participatory sessions, synthesizing findings using grounded theory
  • Developed design patterns and interaction principles for:
    • collaborative goal-setting
    • assistive robot behavior personalization
    • sustained engagement
  • Informed how the robot adapts to PwMCI motivation, routines, and cognitive needs over time

Key Findings & Impact

  • Collaborative goal-setting drives adherence PwMCI engaged more when goals were co-created and personally meaningful
  • Personalization must reflect real-life routines Embedding interventions in daily contexts improved adoption and long-term use
  • Supportive, non-judgmental interaction builds trust PwMCI preferred encouragement and effort-based feedback over directive or evaluative behavior
  • Bridging clinic → real-world use is critical Systems must help PwMCI apply cognitive strategies beyond structured sessions

This work contributed to peer-reviewed publications and established design foundations for cognitively assistive robots deployed in home environments.

Publications

  • ACM/IEEE International Conference on Human-Robot Interaction (HRI), 2022 Design patterns for translating clinical interventions into robot behaviors
  • Alzheimer’s & Dementia (Journal) 2022 Requirements for longitudinal, home-based neurorehabilitation systems
  • ACM/IEEE International Conference on Human-Robot Interaction (HRI), 2023 Framework for robot-supported collaborative goal-setting

Reflection & Next Steps

This project reshaped my approach to assistive design, from optimizing task completion to designing for long-term dignity, motivation, and real-world integration.

Future work includes expanding in-home longitudinal testing and developing adaptive systems that respond to evolving user motivation and cognitive needs.
