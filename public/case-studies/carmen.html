Context & People

Placeholder image for context and people

CARMEN focused on supporting people with Mild Cognitive Impairment in home-based neurorehabilitation and motivation routines. Stakeholders included patients, caregivers, and clinicians.

A major challenge was designing assistive technology that motivates without feeling intrusive, patronizing, or overly controlling in intimate home environments.

My Method & Rationale

Placeholder image for method and rationale

I conducted participatory co-design sessions and in-depth interviews to capture lived experiences and emotional responses to assistive technologies. Grounded qualitative analysis was used to identify recurring themes and translate them into design implications.

This approach was chosen because traditional lab-only testing would not capture the complexity of home-based care dynamics.

Key Findings & Impact

Placeholder image for findings and impact

Findings showed that users preferred collaborative goal-setting and emotionally supportive interaction styles over directive robotic behavior. These insights informed design patterns for cognitively assistive robots that prioritize dignity, agency, and personalization.

The work contributed to research publications and broader discussions on ethical assistive robotics design.

Reflection & Next Steps

Placeholder image for reflection and next steps

I developed strong skills in co-design facilitation, qualitative synthesis, and stakeholder-sensitive research. I also learned how to communicate complex findings to multidisciplinary teams.

If revisiting the project, I would incorporate more iterative prototype testing in real home environments. Future directions include integrating adaptive personalization based on user motivation patterns.
