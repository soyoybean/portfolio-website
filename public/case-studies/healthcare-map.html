Healthcare Access Systemic Map (Interactive Demo Available)

Interactive system mapping of U.S. healthcare access across stakeholders and barriers

My Role
Led systems research, stakeholder modeling, and interactive prototype development
My Team
Individual project (Lab for Cybernetics)
Timeline
Jan 2026 – Present

Summary

Reveals how decisions propagate through the system, redistributing burden onto patients and caregivers and reframing access as a systems problem.

Context & People

This project examines U.S. healthcare access as a complex socio-technical system, not a single solvable problem.

Stakeholders include:

  • Patients with disabilities
  • Caregivers
  • Clinicians, insurers, and regulators
  • Employers and community organizations

Rather than isolated UX failures, access emerges from interactions between institutions, policies, and structural barriers.

My Contributions

I developed a cybernetic systems model to map how healthcare access is produced across stakeholders.

    • Clickable nodes to trace causal pathways

  • Built features for:
    • System tracing (direct + cascading effects)

    • User-contributed insights (participatory comments)

Key shift:
Reframed the problem from "design better tools" → "understand how systems produce unequal burden."

Key Insights

  • Access is systemically produced Not determined by products alone, but by interactions across institutions
  • Authorizers act as control points Clinicians, insurers, and regulators determine what care becomes accessible
  • Barriers are emergent, not isolated Physical, policy, and economic barriers arise from system interactions
  • Burden is redistributed, not removed Friction is absorbed by patients and caregivers (time, cost, emotional labor)
  • Visibility shapes intervention Mapping the system reveals leverage points for meaningful change

Impact

The interactive demo transforms abstract systemic complexity into a tangible, explorable tool.

  • Enables users to:
    • Trace how decisions propagate across the system
    • Understand where friction and burden accumulate
  • Serves as a:
    • Research artifact
    • Discussion tool
    • Design exploration framework

Reframes accessibility from:
UX problem → system coordination and governance problem
