Context & People

Placeholder image for context and people

This project examined how patients and caregivers navigate the complex U.S. healthcare access system, which involves multiple institutions, policies, and social barriers. Stakeholders included patients, caregivers, and healthcare providers whose experiences are shaped by systemic constraints rather than isolated decisions.

My Method & Rationale

Placeholder image for method and rationale

I synthesized stakeholder narratives and systemic research into an interactive systems map that visualizes access barriers and feedback loops. A systems mapping approach was chosen because linear UX models could not capture the interconnected nature of healthcare access challenges.

Key Findings & Impact

Placeholder image for findings and impact

The project revealed that access barriers are often cumulative and context-dependent rather than single-point failures. By integrating lived experiences into an interactive model, the demo helped communicate complex systemic insights in an accessible and engaging format.

It served as a research artifact for discussion, education, and design exploration.

Reflection & Next Steps

Placeholder image for reflection and next steps

I strengthened my systems thinking, research synthesis, and interactive design skills. I also learned how to translate abstract systemic issues into tangible visual tools.

If revisited, I would incorporate real-time data layers and stakeholder workshops to further validate and refine the model’s accuracy and applicability.
