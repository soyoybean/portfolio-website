Context & People

Placeholder image for context and people

This project addressed the experiences of students with academic accommodation needs who often face emotional, bureaucratic, and communication barriers when advocating for themselves. Stakeholders included students with disabilities, institutional staff, and educators.

A major pain point was the psychological burden and uncertainty in formal communication processes, where students feared stigma, misunderstanding, or rejection.

My Method & Rationale

Placeholder image for method and rationale

I led usability-focused research using scenario-based tasks and qualitative interviews to evaluate how users interacted with an AI advocacy assistant. The rationale for this approach was that sensitive institutional communication requires understanding emotional trust, not just task completion metrics.

I focused on evaluating user perceptions of control, privacy, and psychological safety when interacting with AI-generated advocacy support.

Key Findings & Impact

Placeholder image for findings and impact

Key findings revealed that users preferred AI assistance that enhanced clarity and confidence without removing personal agency. Overly automated messaging reduced trust, while customizable outputs increased perceived empowerment.

These insights informed design recommendations for ethical AI systems in sensitive contexts, particularly around transparency, consent, and tone control. The project contributed to research on responsible AI and human-centered support tools in institutional settings.

Reflection & Next Steps

Placeholder image for reflection and next steps

This project strengthened my ability to research ethically sensitive topics and communicate findings in psychologically nuanced contexts. I learned the importance of balancing automation with user autonomy in AI systems.

If I were to improve the study, I would include longitudinal testing to observe real-world adoption behavior. Future steps include expanding evaluation across diverse institutional contexts to test scalability and inclusivity.
