FlowGuard

Assistive robotic system for chronic pain relief in long-term wheelchair users

My Role
UX Research & Co-design, Hardware Engineering
Team
2 Design Researchers, 2 Engineers, 2 Client Advisors (Henry & Jane Evans).
Timeline
May 2023 – Present

Summary

Deployed in real-world use 12+ hours daily for more than a year, reducing both pain and caregiver burden through co-designed, low-effort interaction.

Context & People

Through a series of virtual interviews, a clear challenge emerged:

Henry experienced chronic pain but had limited autonomy in accessing consistent physical relief.

Jane, however, had trouble explaining what she wanted – “Oh if only you guys were here to see it!”

Without hesitation, I convinced my team to fly to their house, where we conducted direct observations, contextual inquiries, and participatory research.

Throughout the day, Henry would sporadically experience intense and chronic pain, often in forms of spasms. Each time, Henry would ping Jane via his mobile robot, and Jane would manually massage Henry’s legs, but this did not guarantee pain relief.

The design opportunity became clear:

Create a relief system that supports autonomy while fitting naturally into caregiver routines.

My Contributions

Given the sensitive and embodied nature of the problem, we prioritized qualitative and participatory approaches. Leading a team of four students, I directed:

  • field study
    • Contextual inquiries
    • Direct observations
  • Participatory co-design with Henry and Jane
  • Iterative usability testing

I also built the working prototype using Arduino hardware and custom interaction logic, iterating directly based on user feedback.

Impact

In February 2025, we installed the final prototype on Henry’s wheelchair, and since then, we have been gathering extremely positive feedback:

Reliable relief

“It's exactly what I had in mind.”

Reduced caregiver burden

“You just took one thing off my plate.”

Simple, intuitive use

“Plugging and charging — it’s just like a cellphone.”

Reflection & Next Steps

This project strengthened my ability to translate sensitive user insights into tangible design decisions, particularly in health-related contexts.

Next steps would include:

  • Recruiting users with diverse mobility profiles earlier in the process
  • Running longitudinal testing across multiple users
  • Collaborating with clinicians and rehabilitation specialists

Thank you, Henry and Jane, for your generous patience and collaboration with us.

"Despite everything, it's still you."
